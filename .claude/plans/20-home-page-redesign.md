# Home Page Redesign

**Goal:** Redesign the home page from the ground up, driven by user stories that reflect what users most often need when they open the app.

**Approach:** Define and refine user stories first, then derive the design from them — not the other way around.

---

## Phase 1: User Stories

### High-frequency (every visit)

1. **"I just had a conversation and want to log it before I forget."**
   This is likely the #1 reason someone opens the app. They talked to someone and want to capture notes + any action items that came out of it.

2. **"I want to see what I need to do today."**
   Check action items that are due or overdue — things I promised people I'd do.

3. **"I want to see who I should reach out to."**
   Relationships going stale. People I haven't talked to in a while relative to how often I want to stay in touch.

### Medium-frequency

4. **"I completed an action item and want to check it off."**
   Quick in-and-out to mark something done.

5. **"I want to send a follow-up to someone but don't know what to say."**
   Looking for help drafting an outreach message.

6. **"I want a quick pulse on my network health."**
   Am I keeping up with my relationships overall, or am I falling behind?

### Low-frequency

7. **"I met someone new and want to add them."**
   Adding a new contact after a networking event or introduction.

8. **"I want to see AI suggestions for who to reconnect with."**
   Proactive nudges beyond just cadence-based reminders.

---

## Open Questions

- Does this priority ordering match real usage? Is #1 really the most common action?
- Are there stories here that don't belong on the *home page* specifically (maybe they belong on other pages)?
- Any stories missing — things a user would want to do on the home page that aren't captured?
- Who is the target user — someone with 20 contacts or 200? Casual networker or power networker?

This guide catalogs the complete surface area across five specimens: the web app, the intelligence & automation engine that powers it, the Chrome extension, the AI-operator MCP server, and the platform & data layer underneath.

Surface II: Intelligence & Automation the engine under the app

The systems that keep the network alive

Beneath the UI: AI drafting and extraction, reply-aware send automation, budget-capped LinkedIn enrichment, career-change detection, and a weekly discovery engine: every one guarded by rate limits, spend caps, and graceful failure.

AI drafting & suggestions

Draft intro email

Subject + HTML body for a first outreach, tone-adjusted for cold vs. warm and grounded in how you met, your goal, and past interactions.

AI

Draft follow-up sequence

Exactly three reply-in-thread follow-ups, progressively shorter and spaced ~7/14/21 days: check-in → add-value → gentle final bump.

AI

General AI composer

Free-form "write me an email about X" pulling in contact context and selected meeting notes, with an auto-generated subject line.

AI

"Up Next" suggestion engine

Blends rule-based generators (graduation proximity, cadence miss, relationship decay, waiting-on reminders) with an LLM pass that mines history for a specific, timely reason to reach out.

AI

Article-share pipeline

An agentic chain: extract a contact's interests → search the web (Serper) → loop an LLM evaluator ("would a real person forward this?") → write a warm reconnection email referencing the find.

AI

Grounded, human-sounding drafts

A 32KB context budget keeps quality high; the prompt blocks generic phrases like "I stumbled upon" so emails sound like you wrote them.

Send automation & deliverability

Follow-up cron

Every 15 minutes: sends due follow-up steps, checks each thread for a reply and cancels the whole sequence if found, sends at most one step per tick, and respects the daily cap.

15 min

Scheduled-email cron

Every 15 minutes: sends due scheduled emails across all users, isolating per-user failures and reporting throughput/delay telemetry.

15 min

Daily send cap

A 100/day Gmail send limit protects deliverability; the guard is re-checked on every send path, app and MCP alike.

Guardrail

Bounce detection & refusal

Gmail delivery failures (NDRs) are parsed to flag bounced addresses, cancel their pending sequences, and refuse them everywhere: draft, send, schedule, sequence.

Guardrail

Reply-aware graduation

When a prospect or archived contact replies, they graduate into the active network and their follow-up clock resets.

Contact-email sync

A resumable, time-budgeted pass pulls 90 days of Gmail history per contact and thread-matches inbound replies to fire the reply-received signal.

Gmail

LinkedIn enrichment & change detection

On-demand enrichment

Re-scrape one contact's LinkedIn profile or find their email via Apify: idempotent, debounced, and monthly-cap-gated, completing async via webhook.

Apify

Daily cadence refresh

Each day, per user, the ~80 stalest contacts are batch-scraped within spend caps so profile data stays current on its own; bundle-imported contacts refresh centrally instead, never from your budget.

Daily

Monthly spend caps & kill switches

A hard monthly USD cap, a softer cadence cap, and per-account enrichment/diff/discovery switches keep paid scraping bounded and controllable.

Guardrail

Change-event diff engine

Compares before/after snapshots into tiered events: promotion, company change, boomerang, open-to-work and hiring flips, location change, new certification, all with false-positive guards.

Work-anniversary events

Detects whole-year anniversaries this month and surfaces a "congratulate them" touchpoint, throttled and limited to your active network.

Change-event feed & dismiss

Surfaceable events for your active network flow into "Up Next" (Tier 1 outranks Tier 2); dismissals are permanent via stable dedupe keys; prospect job changes still badge company pages.

Discovery & analytics

Weekly discovery engine

For opted-in users, searches the stalest high-priority target companies for PMs who joined in the last 90 days: the warmest cold outreach there is.

Weekly

Candidate dedupe & ingest

New profiles are deduped against existing contacts, tombstones, and known candidates before surfacing; people you already know never appear.

Add or dismiss candidates

One click adds a candidate as an auto-enriched prospect at the right company; one click dismisses them forever.

Cross-surface event registry

One typed vocabulary shared by web, server, MCP, and extension, keyed to a north-star metric: replies received.

Outcome analytics with first-party mirror

Every AI draft records whether it was sent as-is, edited (and how much), or discarded; sends, replies, and meetings mirror into CareerVine's own database so data outlives any vendor.

Activation milestones

Fires once per user at contacts-5 and companies-emailed-5, with internal-user activity filtered out of product metrics.

Cross-cutting guardrails. Every route is wrapped with auth, schema validation, per-route hourly rate limits, and awaited analytics. AI features route through a "bring-your-own-key or shared-key" runner with a 24-hour trial and graceful degradation. When a key is out of quota, rejected, or missing, the feature says exactly what's wrong with a one-click path to fix it, never a spinner that never resolves.

Surface III: Chrome Extension LinkedIn → CRM in one click

Import anyone without copy-paste

A Manifest V3 extension that scrapes a LinkedIn profile, has AI structure it into clean fields, lets you review and edit in a side panel, and saves it as a CareerVine contact, all inside a closed Shadow DOM that can't fight the host page.

Floating action button

A CareerVine vine icon appears only on LinkedIn profile pages; one click toggles the side panel open.

Shadow-DOM side panel

The full React panel app renders in a closed Shadow DOM with all: initial isolation, so LinkedIn's CSS can't leak in and the extension can't break LinkedIn.

Profile scraping & cleaning

Waits for lazy-loaded sections, then aggressively strips pronouns, follower counts, activity, and UI junk down to Header, About, Experience, Education, so the AI parse is accurate and cheap.

Profile photo capture

Grabs a real 400×400 photo with a three-tier fallback (rewrite from 100×100, graceful null for ghost avatars) so the saved contact is recognizable.

AI-structured parsing

Cleaned text returns as structured fields: name, location, industry, dated experience, education, status, suggested tags, and generated notes.

AI

"Already in CareerVine" check

Every profile is checked by LinkedIn URL before scraping; a match shows the existing contact and offers "Refresh from LinkedIn" instead of a blind re-import.

Review & edit before saving

Every field is editable (status toggle, follow-up cadence, add/remove experience and education rows with month/year pickers), and edits persist in the per-profile cache.

Save to CareerVine

One click creates or updates the contact with photo; the header switches to a deep link into the web app.

Auto-analyze mode

An opt-in toggle that scrapes and parses each profile automatically as you browse, for power users batching profiles.

Two-hour per-profile cache

Parsed profiles, photos, and edits cache per LinkedIn slug so revisits load instantly with no redundant AI cost or cross-tab collisions.

SPA navigation tracking

Detects LinkedIn's client-side route changes and re-checks the DB, cache, and auto-analyze state as you click around without full reloads.

Progress UI & cooldown

Staged progress ("Reading profile…", "Enriching…") with a 30-second scrape cooldown that protects LinkedIn and the backend.

Toolbar popup

A home base with sign-in, an Import tab (live "is this a profile?" indicator + quick-start), and a Recent tab listing your last imports with relative timestamps.

Secure session handling

Auth tokens live only in the background service worker, auto-refresh with a single-flight guard, and never touch the content script or page, so it stays XSS-resistant.

Security

Graceful failure states

Specific, friendly panels for no/invalid/quota-exhausted AI key and hourly import limits, mirroring the web app's copy with a one-click fix path.

Surface IV: MCP Server 27 tools · an AI operator for your network

Hand your whole network to Claude

An OAuth-protected Model Context Protocol server lets an AI assistant research contacts, write and send grounded Gmail, work the outreach queue, and manage meetings, under the exact same guardrails as the app. The same tools run locally over stdio for development. By design it exposes data and actions only; Claude is the generator, so there are no AI-generation tools.

search_contacts

Search the network by name, email, company, title, school, industry, or tag; filter by tier, returns compact rows with stage and last-touch.

get_contact_dossier

Everything known about one person in one document: the grounding Claude reads before writing an email, including email provenance and history.

add_contact

Create a contact with emails, phones, company, title, school, and location, find-or-creating entities to avoid duplicates.

add_contact_note

Append a timestamped note straight from conversation.

tag_contact

Apply one or more tags, creating any that are missing.

set_network_status

Manually move a contact between active, prospect, and bench tiers.

send_email

Sends immediately, but requires confirm: true, enforces the daily cap and bounce refusal, and logs an interaction.

Queue an email for a future ISO timestamp, delivered by the app's cron.

Create a to-do or a "waiting-on" item linked to one or more contacts.

Calendar

Two shells, one implementation. The hosted server at /api/mcp is an OAuth 2.0 protected resource verifying Supabase-issued JWTs, scoped per request and rate-limited to 100 calls/hour/user. The local careervine-mcp/ package runs the identical 27 tools over stdio for development. A dedicated OAuth consent screen shows the requesting client and exact scopes before any access is granted, and there are no delete tools anywhere.

Surface V: Platform & Data accounts, keys, bundles, admin

Authentication, a bring-your-own-key cost model, living data subscriptions, an admin control plane, and the content pipeline that seeds new users with a real network instead of an empty page.

Data subscriptions

free, living bundles: never a paywall

Subscribe to curated lists

Living updates

Auto-sync

Fill-empty merge

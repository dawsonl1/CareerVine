Getting started

CareerVine, by the job you are doing

CareerVine is a personal networking CRM built on one insight: a conversation, not a contact, is the atomic unit of a relationship. This guide is organized not by where a button lives, but by what you are trying to get done.

Eight jobs, from the empty first day to a job search you run like a pipeline. Each names the capabilities that deliver it and where they live: the web app, the automation engine, the Chrome extension, or the AI-assistant server.

Job 01 day one, not month one

Start with a network, not a blank page#

The hardest part of networking is the empty CRM. CareerVine hands you a real, working network the day you sign up, three ways: a guided first run that ends with a sent email, curated contact lists that keep themselves current, and one-click capture of anyone on LinkedIn.

Your first sitting, guided

ends with a real intro sent, no AI key required#
1

Begin with a network, not an empty page

New accounts are offered a curated recruiting database (real PMs, recruiters, and BYU alumni at companies that hire new grads) instead of a blank CRM.

2

Connect Gmail and Calendar

A dedicated step to link Gmail, so your first email can send and its follow-ups cancel themselves on a reply, and Google Calendar, so you can drop your open times into any message. Skippable, but a deliberate stop so you are set up before you start reaching out.

3

Browse companies while the import streams

The company list opens instantly, ranked by how many alumni you would know there with alumni and product-role sub-counts. A progress bar at the top tracks the import running in the background.

4

Pick a target company

Selecting unlocks the moment the import finishes, so the company page you land on is fully populated. Your first target is the warmest one.

5

Send a personalized intro

A pre-written, editable intro email (with an automatic alumni-to-alumni variant, built from templates so no AI key is needed) sends with follow-ups already scheduled that cancel themselves on a reply.

6

A finale that points forward

"Your first networking email is on its way," plus a recap of what is next: the extension, personalized suggestions, one-click availability. Every step is skippable and resumes across sessions.

Not into the curated list? Declining opens a lighter three-item start instead: add one contact, target one company, connect Gmail. You reach the same place, your own way.

Curated lists that stay current

free, living, never a paywall#

Subscribe to a curated list

Import a published bundle (say, every IB analyst at the NYC boutiques plus the banks and their offices) straight into contacts as ready-to-work prospects.

Free

It stays alive

When a list is re-published with new people or corrected emails, you receive the changes automatically within minutes. No re-import, no stale data.

Auto-sync

Your edits are safe

The merge only ever fills in blanks. Nothing you have edited, tagged, or annotated is overwritten by an update.

Leave on your terms

Unsubscribe and keep everything, or remove only the contacts you never touched. Anyone you have emailed or edited always stays.

Import that survives a closed tab

A chunked, cursor-driven copy with a live progress bar, backed by a background worker and a daily safety-net sweep so a mid-import tab close never loses your place.

Safety net

Capture anyone from LinkedIn

Manifest V3 Chrome extension#

One click on a profile

A CareerVine vine icon appears only on LinkedIn profile pages; one click opens a side panel that turns the person you are viewing into a contact.

Extension

Clean scrape, structured by AI

It strips pronouns, follower counts, and UI junk down to the real profile, then hands the cleaned text to CareerVine's parser, which returns structured fields, suggested tags, and generated notes.

AI

No accidental duplicates

Every profile is checked by LinkedIn URL before scraping. A match shows the existing contact and offers "Refresh from LinkedIn" instead of a blind re-import.

Review and edit before saving

Every field is editable (status, follow-up cadence, experience and education rows with month/year pickers) and your edits persist in a per-profile cache.

Auto-analyze as you browse

A side-panel toggle scrapes and parses each profile automatically as you move between profiles, for power users batching a list.

Isolated and secure by design

The panel renders in a closed Shadow DOM so LinkedIn's CSS cannot leak in and it cannot break LinkedIn; auth tokens live only in the background worker, never in the page.

Security

Job 03 personal, fast, grounded

Write outreach that sounds like you, in seconds#

Every draft is grounded in what you actually know about the person, tuned to how warm the relationship is, and free of the tells that make an email read as machine-written. You edit, you send, and the follow-up plan is already attached.

Write with AI

Pick a built-in preset (Introduction, Follow-up after meeting, Thank you), a custom template of your own, or a free-form prompt, optionally feeding prior meeting notes for personalization.

AI

Guided intro flow

For a first touch: a short "how you met and your goal" form produces an AI intro, then an editable draft, then an AI follow-up plan that auto-schedules reply-aware follow-ups.

AI

Drafts that sound human

A generous context budget keeps quality high, and the prompt blocks generic tells like "I stumbled upon" so the email reads like you wrote it, not a template.

Insert real availability

Drop genuine open calendar slots (standard, priority, or custom profiles) straight into the email body, computed against your busy calendars.

Compose from anywhere

A single compose modal (To, Cc, Bcc, rich text, contact autocomplete, provenance warnings, schedule-send, auto-saved drafts) is reachable from every surface.

Custom AI templates

Create, edit, and delete your own "Write with AI" prompt templates so they appear alongside the built-in presets.

Job 04 persistence on autopilot

Follow up relentlessly, without lifting a finger#

Most opportunities die in the follow-up. CareerVine sends your sequences on schedule, stops the moment someone replies, and guards your sender reputation so you never look like a spammer.

Follow-up sequence builder

Build or edit a multi-step sequence with per-step delay, send time, subject, and rich body; the AI default is three reply-in-thread nudges spaced about 7, 14, and 21 days, each shorter than the last.

AI

Stops the moment they reply

Each tick checks the thread for a reply and cancels the whole sequence if it finds one. A reply also graduates a prospect into your active network and resets their clock.

Auto-cancel

Scheduled sends

Queue any email for a future time and let the system deliver it, across both the app and the AI assistant.

Scheduled

It runs on its own

A worker fires every 15 minutes to send due follow-up steps and scheduled emails, one step per sequence per tick, isolating any single failure so it never blocks the rest.

Every 15 min

Daily send cap

A 100-per-day Gmail send limit protects your deliverability, re-checked on every send path including cron follow-ups, scheduled sends, and the AI assistant.

Guardrail

Bounce detection and refusal

Gmail delivery failures are parsed to flag bounced addresses, cancel their pending sequences, and refuse them everywhere: draft, send, schedule, and sequence.

Guardrail

Job 06 every employer, its own pipeline

Run your job search like a pipeline#

A target company is more than a logo. Track each employer, and each office, as its own recruiting pipeline: who you know there, what stage you are at, your applications, your interviews, and everything you have learned along the way.

The command center for a company

#

One list, sorted by what's next

Every company you are targeting or already know someone at, in a single list. Each card leads with who you know there (people you know, how many are BYU alumni in product roles, recruiters) and the one next move, so you always know where to spend your time. Re-sort by priority, next-app date, traction, or name.

Filter by stage, then refine

Pipeline-stage chips (Researching, Outreach, Applied, Interviewing, Closed) are the primary filter; a collapsible Filters drawer adds warmth (BYU alum in product), traction, tier, and contacts. Every choice lives in the URL with a live result count, so any view is bookmarkable and shareable.

Chase a company early

Add a company before you know anyone there (name, LinkedIn URL, office in seconds), deduped against every company already in your network.

Per-office pipelines

A location scope makes each office ("Google San Diego") its own targetable pipeline with its own status and notes.

Roster by persona

Current and former employees grouped into your network versus prospects, with persona badges (Alum, Product, Recruiter), BYU-alum flags, outreach stage, and email status.

Track the hunt, stage by stage

#

Stage stepper

Researching, Active outreach, Applied, Interviewing, Closed. Each stage is an expandable editor with a collapsed summary and autosave.

Applications and documents

Track multiple applications per company with role, location, date, and resume plus cover-letter PDF uploads.

Interview rounds and cycles

Log interview rounds (date, interviewer, questions); "prepare for next cycle" starts a fresh application cycle that keeps each season separate.

Recruiting-intel log

A timestamped notes log for application windows, referral programs, and team info, so everything you learn on a call lands somewhere you will find it again.

Per-person outreach stage

Separate from the company pipeline, each contact carries a derived outreach stage (not contacted, contacted, replied, call scheduled, referral) reflecting where that one relationship stands.

Work targets one at a time

a focused outreach queue#

Company-at-a-time queue

Step through target companies with the arrow keys (ordered by application deadline then priority), showing "Company X of N" so you never lose your place.

Context at a glance

Logo, priority, program, next-app window, latest intel note, and links to the full company page and bench.

People cards

Current and collapsible former employees with persona, alum, and stage badges, an adjacency score, and a "why they were kept" review note.

The full picture in a modal

Every role, education, and email source for a contact, with Write email, Open profile, and a "Mark contacted" for off-platform outreach.

Nothing hides silently

Companies with only bench or no contactable people are skipped, but with a visible count and a link so you can always look.

Job 07 27 tools for Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor and more

Hand your whole network to an AI assistant#

Connect CareerVine to Claude, the Claude Code CLI, ChatGPT, Codex, or Cursor over a secure server. Your assistant can research a contact, draft and send grounded Gmail, work the outreach queue, and manage meetings, all under the exact same guardrails as the app. By design it never generates on its own: your assistant is the writer, so there are no AI tools, and there are no delete tools anywhere.

Contacts and research

#

search_contacts

Search the network by name, email, company, title, school, industry, or tag; filter by tier, returns compact rows with stage and last-touch.

get_contact_dossier

Everything known about one person in one document: the grounding your assistant reads before writing an email, including email provenance and history.

add_contact

Create a contact with emails, phones, company, title, school, and location, find-or-creating entities to avoid duplicates.

add_contact_note

Append a timestamped note straight from conversation.

tag_contact

Apply one or more tags, creating any that are missing.

set_network_status

Manually move a contact between active, prospect, and bench tiers.

Email

drafts by default, sends are confirm-gated#

create_email_draft

The default path: a real Gmail draft (markdown to HTML, thread-aware) for your review; refuses bounced addresses.

send_email

Sends immediately, but requires confirm: true, enforces the daily cap and bounce refusal, and logs an interaction.

Confirm-gated

schedule_email

Queue an email for a future timestamp, delivered by the app's worker.

create_follow_up_sequence

Attach timed follow-ups to a sent thread that auto-cancel the moment the contact replies.

list_scheduled

List pending scheduled emails and active sequences with next send times.

cancel_scheduled

Cancel a pending scheduled email or an entire follow-up sequence.

search_email_history

Full-text search over cached Gmail, optionally scoped to one contact.

get_email_thread

Live-fetch full message bodies for a thread for reply context.

Outreach engine

#

list_outreach_queue

The company-by-company queue, ordered by deadline then priority, with a "why" reason per entry.

list_companies

Companies in the network with people counts, target status, priority, and traction.

get_company

The full company picture: roster, offices, target status, and the recruiting-intel log.

add_company_intel

Append a timestamped recruiting note, auto-promoting the company to a target.

set_stage_override

Manually set a contact's outreach stage for off-platform activity like a LinkedIn DM.

Relationship upkeep

#

log_interaction

Record a touchpoint that graduates prospects and resets the follow-up clock.

create_action_item

Create a to-do or a "waiting-on" item linked to one or more contacts.

list_action_items

Open items filtered by due window, direction, or contact, with age for waiting-on items.

update_action_item

Complete, snooze, reschedule, or edit an item.

list_due_followups

The "who should I reach out to today" list, most-overdue first.

get_network_health

The "how am I doing" dashboard: streak, on-track ratio, most-neglected contacts, per-tier counts, 30-day activity.

Calendar

#

list_meetings

Upcoming events from the synced calendar with attendees matched to contacts and Meet or Zoom links.

create_meeting

Create a Google Calendar event with an invite and optional Meet link; linking it graduates a prospect.
